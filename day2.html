Day 2: Do we need this?

Day 2: Do we need this?



Script

Ken: Are, kaimasu ka. あれ、買いますか。

Henry: Ee, kaimasu kedo. ええ、買いますけど….

Ken: Kore wa? これは？

Henry: Aa, sore mo chotto irimasu ne. ああ、それもちょっといりますね。

Ken: Will you buy that? あれ、買いますか。

Henry: Yes, I will, but… ええ、買いますけど….

Ken: How about this? これは？

Henry: Oh, we need a few of those, too, don't we? ああ、それもちょっといりますね。

Vocabulary
  • are あれ - that
  • kaimasu かいます・買います - buy
  • kedo けど - but
  • ga が - but
  • kore これ - this
  • wa は - (particle) of contrast
  • sore それ - that near you
  • mo も - (particle) of Addition
  • chotto ちょっと - little bit, a few
  • irimasu いります - need
  • ga が - but (more formal than kedo)
  • takusan たくさん - a lot
  • mimasu みます・見ます - look, watch
  • tsukaimasu つかいます・使います - use
  • kikimasu ききます・聞きます - listen, ask
  • yomimasu よみます・読みます - read
  • kakimasu かきます・書きます - write, draw
  • hanasimasu はなします・話します - talk, speak
Grammar
1. Noun + Verb

In Japanese, subject, object, and other elements are often not explicitly mentioned when it's obvious from context. When it's not clear, you can place them before the verb.:

  • Subject: Honda-san wa nomimasu ka? - "Does Ms. Honda drink?"
  • Object: Kore tsukaimasu ka? - "We're going to use this, right?"

The common word order is: Time - Subject - Object - Adverb - Verb.

Example: Kyou, watashi wa kore wo tukaimasu. - "Today, I'm going to use this."

2. Ko-so-a-do series

Japanese uses a three-way distinction for referring to things:

  • ko-re: this thing (something close to me), or this thing I just mentioned
  • so-re: that thing (something close to you), or that thing that was just mentioned
  • a-re: that thing (something away from both of us), or that thing we both know about
  • do-re: "which one?"
  • ko-ko: here (somewhere close to me), or this place I just mentioned
  • so-ko: there (somewhere close to you), or that place that was just mentioned
  • a-soko: that place (somewhere away from both of us), or that place we both know about
  • do-ko: "where?"
  • ko-no: this (+ noun), or this [noun] I just mentioned
  • so-no: that (+ noun), or that [noun] that was just mentioned
  • a-no: that (+ noun) (somewhere away from both of us), or that [noun] we both know about
  • do-no: "which (+ noun)?"
3. Clause Particle "Kedo/Ga"

"Kedo/Ga" = 'but' connects two sentences ("clause particle"). It can express contrasting ideas or serve as an introduction:

  • Kore wa kaimasu kedo, a-re wa kaimasen. - "I'll buy this, but I won't buy that."
  • Sumimasen-ga, wakarimasen. - "I'm sorry but I don't understand."
4. Particle "Wa" indicating Contrast

Particle "wa" follows nouns and indicates contrast. It can be used with subjects, objects, etc.:

  • Watashi wa mimasu kedo… - "I watch it, but… (someone else may not)"
  • Kore wa wakarimasu. - "I understand this (but not the other one)"
5. Particle "Mo" indicates Addition

The particle "mo" means 'too' or 'also' with an affirmative verb and '(n)either' with a negative verb:

  • Honda-san mo mimasu. - "Ms. Honda watches it, too." (as well as someone else)
  • Kore mo wakarimasen. - "I don't understand this, either." (in addition to something else)
