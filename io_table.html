World Input-Output Table

Increasing global integration through international trade and technological development is linked to an increasing inequality between countries as well as between various classes within society. Any analysis of their causes and effects needs to recognize the dynamic interrelatedness of countries and industries. The World Input Output Database (WIOD) project (which ran from May 1, 2009 to May 1, 2012) has developed new databases, accounting frameworks and models to increase our understanding of the above-mentioned phenomena. The core of the database is a set of harmonized supply and use tables, alongside with data on international trade in goods and services. Researchers in the European Commission created the international Input-Output table that captures the exchange of value-added among the EU countries and some major economies in the world.

which provides time-series data of the intermediate input values delivered across sectors worldwide at current prices, denoted in millions of U.S. dollars. The database covers 27 EU countries and 14 major countries in the world for the period from 1995 to 2010. For each country, the database details intermediate input and final output for 35 industries including agriculture, construction, 14 manufacturing industries, business services and more. The database was constructed by systematically merging various national accounts data including ISWGNA (Intersecretariat Working Group on National Accounts) and international trade statistics. The driving value of the WIOT is that it is specifically constructed to track development patterns over time using a time-series table of value-added, trade, and import/export metrics. Through these metrics, it better captures the trade participation of each country by tracking the value-added at each stage of production including intermediaries as well as the value-added by suppliers more upstream in the production process.
